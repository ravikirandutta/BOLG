    Nash Equilibrium : A place where neither player wants to change strategy unilaterally(YY).If all players have a dominant strategy, then there can be only one Nash equilibrium - the outcome where all players choose their dominant strategy. If there is another outcome where both players have better outcome(XX), then the current outcome is 'Pareto inefficient'. An outcome is Pareto efficient if moving one player to another outcome worsens the outcome for the other player.

      Entry and ExitEntrants threaten incumbents by stealing market share as well intensifying competition (price) resulting in a reduction of the overall profit pies. Exit can have the opposite effect.

Summary of Entry and Exit research1. Entry will be pervasive. 2. Entrants and exiters tend to be smaller than established players3. Most entrants do not survive more than 10 years but those that do grow precipitously.4. Entry and exit rates vary by industry

Structural Entry Barriers1. Control of essential resources - for production Ex De Beers in diamonds . This runs the risk of alternate production resources or legislation issues.2. Economies of scale and scope - create entry barriers by forcing the entrants to enter on a large scale or with many products to achieve unit cost parity with incumbent firms.3.  Marketing advantages of incumbents

Blockaded Entry : Entry is blockaded if structural barriers are too high. For example Semiconductor fabrication businAccommodated Entry : If entry barriers are low then incumbents adapt this strategy since the cost of preventing entry is greater than the benefitsDeterred Entry: Employing entry deterring strategies Strategic Entry BarriersEntry deterring strategies1. Limit pricing : Reducing prices before entry occurs2. Predatory pricing : Setting prices low  enough to drive out other firms.Predatory and Limit usually don’t work much in theory. However uncertainty of post entry pricing is what helps these pricing strategies.3. Excess capacity - Maintain excess capacity with the intent to reduce prices if entry occurs

    Economies of scale and scope create barriers for entry because they force potential entrants to enter on a large scale or with many products to achieve unit cost parity with incumbent firms.Entering at a large scale or scope is only disadvntageous to the extent that entrant cannot recover its upfront costs if it subsequently decides to exit. An entrant whose upfront cost is not sunk, can come at large scale, undercut the incumbent firms prices and exit the market and recover its entry costs if incumbent firms retaliate.This strategy is known as "bit and run entry"will leave incumbents vulnerable to entry even if economies of scale is significant. Hence Sunk costs not economies of scale are true barriers to entry.

Pros:

Cons:
o   Lose flex. And focus
o   Heavier capital requirement and entry costs
o   Higher exit barriers
o   Need to develop other tech. and mgmt. skills for new production
o   A strong position on the value chain does not guarantee a strong position elsewhere
